Bruce Lee - Movies

Bruce Lee's film career began in earnest in the 1970s, when he starred in a series of action films that showcased his unique style of martial arts. His films were huge hits and helped to popularize martial arts in the West.

Enter the Dragon

"Enter the Dragon" is a 1973 martial arts film directed by Robert Clouse. The film stars Bruce Lee, John Saxon and Jim Kelly. It would be Bruce Lee's final completed film appearance before his death on 20 July 1973 at age 32. Often considered the ultimate kung-fu movie and a fitting swan song for Bruce Lee. The film's appeal rests almost entirely on Lee's fight scenes. Critics have praised the film for its intense fight sequences and its ability to entertain without relying just on martial arts. It has been described as a pop culture gem of a film, watching "Enter the Dragon" is like watching a comic book come to life but without any of the superpowers.

Fists of Fury

"Fists of Fury" is a 1971 Hong Kong martial arts film directed by Lo Wei, starring Bruce Lee in his first major film. It was his first leading role in a major film, after a series of minor roles in U.S. television shows. Despite being seen as a routine exploitation movie with a rudimentary revenge plot, hilariously bad dialogue, amateurish acting, and primitive cinematic technique, it is quite enjoyable as a display of Bruce Lee's martial arts skills.

The Way of the Dragon

"The Way of the Dragon" is a 1972 Hong Kong martial arts action comedy film written, produced and directed by Bruce Lee, who also stars in the lead role. This is Bruce Lee's only complete directorial film.
