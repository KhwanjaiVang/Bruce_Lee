Bruce Lee - Biography

Bruce Lee was born on November 27, 1940, in the Chinatown area of San Francisco, California. He was a Hong Kong-American actor, director, martial artist, and philosopher. He is widely considered one of the most influential martial artists of all time and a pop culture icon of the 20th century.

Early Life

Bruce Lee was born Lee Jun-fan in San Francisco, California. His father, Lee Hoi-chuen, was a famous Cantonese opera star. Lee was introduced to the film industry by his father and appeared in several films as a child actor. His early martial arts experience included Wing Chun (trained under Ip Man), tai chi, boxing (winning a Hong Kong boxing tournament), and frequent street fighting (neighborhood and rooftop fights).

Martial Arts Career

Bruce Lee began training in martial arts at the age of 13. He developed his own martial arts philosophy and training program, which he called Jeet Kune Do (The Way of the Intercepting Fist). From 1959 until 1964, he gave up his acting career to pursue a vocation in martial arts. He started off as a teacher of Kung Fu. With time, he opened his own martial arts school, Lee Jun Fan Gung Fu Institute, in Seattle.

Film Career

Bruce Lee's film career began in earnest in the 1970s, when he starred in a series of action films that showcased his unique style of martial arts. His films, including "Enter the Dragon" and "Fists of Fury," were huge hits and helped to popularize martial arts in the West. After The Green Hornet series was canceled, Bruce encountered resistance while working in Hollywood and so headed to Hong Kong to pursue a film career. In Hong Kong he made 3 films, which consecutively broke all box office records and showcased martial arts in an entirely new way.
