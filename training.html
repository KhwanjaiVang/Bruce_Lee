Bruce Lee - Training

Bruce Lee was known for his intense and dedicated training regimen. His physical fitness and agility were among the best in the world.

Training Type Description
Physical Training Bruce Lee's physical training included weight training, cardiovascular training, flexibility training, and nutrition. He believed in functional strength and endurance over muscle mass. His workout routine consisted of running four to five miles each morning and weightlifting three nights a week. He had a comprehensive home gym with equipment like a squat rack, bench press, dumbbells, and grip machine.
Martial Arts Training Bruce Lee trained in several styles of martial arts, but he is most well known for founding Jeet Kune Do, a hybrid martial arts philosophy drawing from different combat disciplines. He was a true proponent of embracing various martial arts and fundamental training programs. His openness and flexibility to experiment with different fighting and training methods birthed his very own style, Jeet Kune Do.
Mental Training Bruce Lee also believed in the importance of mental training in martial arts. He often incorporated meditation and other mental exercises into his training regimen. He was always looking for ways to maximize his potential.
Evolution of Bruce Lee's Training Over time, Bruce Lee's training methods evolved. In 1963, his training methods were mostly done in a traditional fashion. He would spend 3 or more hours training on a wooden dummy and spend several days performing techniques of the first form of Wing Chun. By 1965, Bruce Lee began a cardiovascular routine in order to increase his endurance and stamina. His cardio exercises consisted of cycling, running, and jump roping.
